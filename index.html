What I do for fun

Rock Climbing

My buddies and I bought intro rock climbing classes the week of the inital Covid lockdown. Once they opened back up, we went up and never wanted to come back down. Climbing has significantly influenced my coding abilities due to their shared characteristics. Breaking down challenging routes into smaller, manageable segments is like breaking down big chunks of code into smaller, easier-to-understand parts. Furthermore, the collaborative nature of climbing, requiring teamwork and constructive feedback, mirrors the dynamics of successful software development teams.
We've all progressed throughout the years climbing top rope and lead, and currently climb grades 5.11 A's and up. Even if you didn't like the site and just wanna climb, shoot me a message and I'll meet you up on some tall walls.

Biking

Biking is one of my true passions. I ride a fixed gear bike and I will not stop until my knees give out. I used to do a lot of solo rides, but in the past year I have been riding in groups, which has showed me how to adapt to others and how effectively communicate on the fly. One of the main reasons why I love cycling is the challange to overcome, hitting a hill or a sprint with all your might is very reminisent to the need and want to overcome obstacles in programming; just as when you are biking persistence and determination is key when you're trying to debug a code issue or encountering and frustrating situation (in which there are always many!)
