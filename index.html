Projects

Legit or Bit

In many developing nations, cellphone users often access the internet through subsidized plans offered by companies like Meta. These plans are attractive due to their low costs, providing unlimited access to Meta’s services while offering minimal data for other applications. But is this truly a benefit, or is it a way to control and limit access?
To address this, I developed a WhatsApp bot that enables users to fact-check and find related information for any news article link or text submission, all without leaving the WhatsApp app. This project was made possible with the support of Twilio, Claimbusters, and the Google Claims API.

Nyc's Fastest Strava Bikers

I’ve always been frustrated with Strava’s site, especially how it limits access to the vast amount of data they collect on users' rides. Finding all segments at once is nearly impossible. To address this, I created a page that displays all cycling segments in NYC along with the top 25 riders for each segment. This tool allows users to plan routes to maximize segment visits, aiding them in aiming for sub-25 times to be featured on the main leaderboard page. With just a click, users can access all segments and view engaging data visualizations, including riders with the most sub-25 positions, placement statistics, density charts for top riders, and the times of year when the fastest rides occur. This project helps Strava riders optimize their segment performances to try to be the fastest in NYC.
